About

I am a remote sensing researcher with a background in environmental science, currently working as a Monitoring & Data Analysis Officer at NGO Forum for Public Health in Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh, supporting FAO-funded impact assessments and field surveys.

My graduate research at UNIST's Intelligent Remote Sensing & GeoAI Lab focused on deep learning architectures for post-wildfire burned area mapping using Sentinel-1 SAR and Landsat data — including novel Mamba-based Siamese networks for burn severity estimation.

Previously a Junior Research Fellow at SPARRSO, I developed flood risk and inundation maps using Multi-Criteria Decision Methods and Google Earth Engine. I am driven by applying satellite intelligence to real-world disaster management and climate risk challenges.
