About Me

Mapping the Earth
through Satellite Eyes

I am a remote sensing researcher and environmental scientist who combines satellite data with deep learning to address wildfires, flood risk, and climate-driven land change.

My graduate research at UNIST's Intelligent Remote Sensing & GeoAI Lab produced novel Mamba-based Siamese networks for burn severity estimation from Landsat imagery, and explored U-Net architectures for SAR-based burned area detection using Sentinel-1 data.

I recently joined Esri Bangladesh Ltd. as a Solution Specialist/Analyst — bringing GIS technology solutions to local challenges. I also serve as Monitoring & Data Analysis Officer at NGO Forum for Public Health in Cox's Bazar, translating geospatial skills into measurable humanitarian outcomes for FAO-funded projects.

Actively seeking PhD opportunities in Australia and Europe, with focus on SAR-optical data fusion, deep learning architectures, and vegetation/wildfire monitoring.
