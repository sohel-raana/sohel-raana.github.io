About

I am a remote sensing researcher and environmental scientist who combines satellite data with deep learning to address wildfires, flood risk, and climate-driven land change.

My graduate research at UNIST's Intelligent Remote Sensing & GeoAI Lab produced novel Mamba-based Siamese networks for burn severity estimation from Landsat imagery, and explored U-Net architectures for SAR-based burned area detection using Sentinel-1 data.

Currently, I serve as Monitoring & Data Analysis Officer at NGO Forum for Public Health in Cox's Bazar — translating geospatial skills into measurable humanitarian outcomes for FAO-funded projects supporting Rohingya communities.
