About me

I am a remote sensing researcher and environmental scientist who uses satellite imagery with deep learning to track wildfire severity, flood risk, and land change as it happens.

My graduate research at UNIST's Intelligent Remote Sensing & Geospatial Information Science (IRIS) Lab produced novel Mamba-based Siamese networks for burn severity estimation from Landsat imagery, and explored U-Net architectures for SAR-based burned area detection using Sentinel-1 data.

I recently joined Esri Bangladesh Ltd. as a Solution Analyst, working on geospatial solutions and technical support across the Esri ArcGIS platform. I also served as Monitoring & Data Analysis Officer at NGO Forum for Public Health in Cox's Bazar, translating geospatial skills into measurable humanitarian outcomes for FAO-funded projects.

Actively seeking PhD opportunities in Australia and Europe, with a focus on SAR-optical data fusion, deep learning architectures, and vegetation/wildfire monitoring.

INT'L JOURNAL OF APPLIED EARTH OBSERVATION AND GEOINFORMATION · 2026
Exploring the impacts of dual-polarized vegetation indices and U-shaped deep learning architectures on SAR-based burned area mapping
Rana, S. M. S., Lee, J., Kang, Y., Im, J.
DOI →
Frequent and severe wildfires driven by climate change intensify the need for accurate burned area (BA) mapping, which can be effectively addressed using Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) due to its cloud-penetration capability and sensitivity to vegetation and moisture changes. However, BA mapping based on SAR-only approaches relies on U-Net with ResNet50 backbone or fully convolutional neural network, while the potential of advanced architectural components remains underexplored. This study evaluates five U-Net variants (U-Net, Attention U-Net, Residual Attention U-Net, U-Net++, and U-Net 3+) using four input schemes: log-ratio, log-ratio without cross-ratio, vegetation indices (VIs), and a combined feature set. U-Net++ with log-ratio inputs under BCE loss achieves the highest performance (F1: 0.8218, IoU: 0.6795); VIs alone, with focal loss, reach F1: 0.8244, IoU: 0.7013; combining VIs with log-ratio features under dice and focal loss delivers the best overall performance (F1: 0.8364, IoU: 0.7188). The study offers a quantitative evaluation of how dual-polarized VIs and deep learning architectures affect SAR-based BA mapping and suggests directions for future improvement through advanced feature extraction.
NATURAL HAZARDS RESEARCH · 2024
Flood risk mapping of the flood-prone Rangpur division of Bangladesh using remote sensing and multi-criteria analysis
Rana, S. M. S., Habib, S. A., Sharifee, M. N. H., Sultana, N., Rahman, S. H.
DOI →
Identification of potential flood risk areas is crucial to reduce flood damage for the frequently flooded, low-lying South Asian developing countries. This study prepares a district-level flood risk map for the flood-prone Rangpur Division of Bangladesh using multi-criteria decision analysis with the analytical hierarchy process (AHP) method. Eight physical factors (elevation, slope, distance from river, drainage density, land cover, rainfall, HAND, TWI) and six social factors (population density, dependent population, disabled population, female population, agriculture-dependent population, literacy) were assessed to build a five-tier risk map. 62.46% of the Rangpur Division was found to lie under moderate-to-very-high flood risk. Using ROC-AUC validation against the 2020 Sentinel-1-derived flood inventory, the map scored 0.83 — guiding policymakers toward vulnerable areas and suitable zones for development.
ENGINEERING PROCEEDINGS · 2021 Conference
Analysis of NO₂ pollution over Bangladesh between the two COVID-19 caused lockdowns in 2020 and 2021 using Sentinel-5P products
Rana, S. M. S., Ahmed, S. M. F., Akter, H.
DOI →
During Bangladesh's two COVID-19 lockdowns (26 Mar–30 May 2020; 3 Apr–31 May 2021), this study analysed tropospheric NO₂ concentration using Sentinel-5P data processed with the HARP toolkit and Python, comparing monthly averages against the same 2019 period. NO₂ dropped sharply in April 2020 but rebounded by May; during the lighter-restriction 2021 lockdown, concentrations were markedly higher, with most pollution concentrated in the Dhaka district. The findings highlight how lockdown stringency directly shaped air quality outcomes and point to the value of Sentinel data for ongoing air-quality monitoring.
